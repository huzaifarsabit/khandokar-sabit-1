Pink Floyd

About The Band

Pink Floyd, formed in 1965 in London, started as a psychedelic rock band led by Syd Barrett. After Barrett's departure due to mental health issues, guitarist David Gilmour joined, and bassist Roger Waters emerged as the primary songwriter. The band's music evolved into progressive rock with elaborate live shows and concept albums, including "The Dark Side of the Moon" (1973) and "The Wall" (1979), which gained them international acclaim. Tensions led to Waters leaving in the 1980s, but Gilmour and the remaining members continued to produce music and tour. Despite various lineup changes and legal battles, Pink Floyd's innovative sound and thematic depth have left a lasting impact on rock music.
